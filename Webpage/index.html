Shelter is about a young girl who is the last living human floating in the emptiness of space after a meteor destroyed
the Earth. her father built a spacecraft to allow her to escape. He hooked her up to a simulation where she spends
the rest of her life in a virtual world that she controls. After 7 years of isolation, she discovers her past and what
happened to the world. She receives the last message her father left behind for her. The lyrics are the girl's
conversation with the memory of her father. Her father expresses the pain of leaving her behind, and she
reassures him that she'll be able to move forward without him. The song reflects the loneliness Porter
Robinson and Madeon feel when they are on tour away from home.

Koichi Sato was a Japanese designer who lived from 1944-2016. He is known for his post-WW2
Japanese posters that blend minimalism with luminous, soft color gradients and suspended forms that
feel ethereal. His posters feel weightless and contemplative. I chose Koichi Sato for my inspiration because
his works give off the feeling of drifting in space that Shelter represents.
